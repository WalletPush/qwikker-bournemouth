## [SECTION 2: CONNECT STRIPE — 0:50-2:30]

*Show: Pricing & Billing tab → Payment Processing section*

*Voiceover:*

> The first thing you'll see is **Payment Processing**. This is where you connect your Stripe account. When businesses subscribe to paid tiers, the money goes directly into YOUR Stripe account — Qwikker doesn't take a cut of subscription revenue.

*Action: Click "Connect with Stripe" button*

> Clicking **Connect with Stripe** takes you to Stripe's website. If you already have an account, you'll log in and authorise the connection. If you don't, you'll create one — Stripe walks you through it. You'll need your bank details and some business information.

*Show: Stripe OAuth flow (or screenshot placeholder)*

> Once you've authorised, Stripe redirects you back here. You should see a green **"Stripe Connected"** panel with your account ID. If it says **"onboarding not complete"**, click Connect with Stripe again to finish — sometimes Stripe needs additional verification before you can accept payments.

*Show: Green "Stripe Connected" state with account ID visible*

> Perfect. You can click **View Stripe Dashboard** anytime to see your payments, invoices, and payouts. Now let's set your prices.

## [SECTION 3: PRICING CARD EDITOR — 2:30-5:30]

*Show: Scroll down to Pricing Card Editor section*

### Currency & Tax

*Voiceover:*

> First up is **Currency & Tax Settings**. Choose your currency from the dropdown — if you're in the UK, select GBP and the pound symbol. Set your tax rate — in the UK that's 20% VAT. The tax name is what appears on invoices.

*Action: Show selecting GBP, typing "£", entering 20, typing "VAT"*

### Founding Member Benefit

*Voiceover:*

> Next is the **Founding Member Benefit**. This is optional — if you tick the checkbox, a promotional banner appears above your pricing cards offering early businesses a discount on annual plans. Set your discount percentage and write a short description. This is great for your launch period to incentivise annual commitments.

*Action: Show ticking the checkbox, entering "20" for discount, typing a description*

### Editing Pricing Cards

*Voiceover:*

> Below that is the **Live Preview**. You'll see four pricing cards: **Free**, **Starter**, **Featured**, and **Spotlight**. Click any card to edit it.

*Action: Click the Starter card*

> When you select a card, the editor panel appears. The **plan name is fixed** — you can't rename tiers. But you CAN edit:
>
> - The **subtitle** — a short description of who the plan is for
> - The **monthly and annual prices** — set whatever makes sense for your market
> - The **CTA button text** — what the subscribe button says
> - The **colour scheme** — Slate, Blue, or Gold
> - Whether it's marked as **Most Popular**
> - And the **features list** — the bullet points businesses see

*Action: Show changing the monthly price, editing a feature, clicking "Add Feature"*

> **Important:** The features listed here are marketing copy — they describe what each tier includes, but they don't change what the platform actually enforces. The platform enforces tier limits automatically. So if Starter allows 3 offers, it allows 3 offers regardless of what you write on the card. Keep your feature descriptions accurate.

*Action: Show editing featured card similarly*

> For reference, the default UK prices are:
> - **Starter:** £19.99/month
> - **Featured:** £49.99/month
> - **Spotlight:** £129/month
>
> Adjust for your market. If you're in Southeast Asia, these might be much lower. If you're in London, maybe slightly higher.

*Action: Click "Save & Deploy Pricing Cards"*

> Hit **Save & Deploy** and your pricing is live. Businesses will see these cards when they visit the upgrade section of their dashboard.

## [SECTION 4: TRIAL & ONBOARDING — 5:30-7:00]

*Action: Click the "Trial & Onboarding" tab*

*Voiceover:*

> Next tab — **Trial & Onboarding**. This controls what happens when a new business signs up and chooses to start a free trial.

*Show: Trial Configuration card*

> You have two settings:
>
> **Default Trial Tier** — choose Starter, Featured, or Spotlight. This is the tier that trial businesses get access to. If you choose Spotlight, they'll get everything — loyalty cards, push notifications, the works. If you choose Starter, they only get the Starter features during their trial.

*Action: Show selecting "Spotlight" from dropdown*

> My recommendation: **give them Spotlight**. You want trial businesses to experience the full platform — especially the loyalty card system. That's your strongest conversion tool. If they've had 50 customers join their stamp card during the trial, they won't want to lose it.

> **Trial Duration** — how many days the trial lasts. Minimum is 7, maximum is 365. The default is 30 days, but you can go longer for your launch period.

*Action: Show entering "60" for trial duration*

> For your initial launch, I'd suggest **60 days**. It gives businesses enough time to properly use the loyalty system, run offers, and see real results. You can always shorten it later once you have proof the platform works.

*Action: Click "Save Trial Configuration"*

> Save, and you're done. Every new business that signs up and chooses "Free Trial" will get these settings applied automatically.

## [SECTION 5: LANDING PAGE EDITOR — 7:00-10:30]

*Action: Click the "Landing Page" tab*

*Voiceover:*

> Last tab — your **Landing Page**. This is what people see when they visit your city's URL — like `bournemouth.qwikker.com`. Let's make it look professional.

### Hero Section

*Show: Hero Section card*

> The **Hero Section** is the first thing visitors see. You can customise the headline and subtitle — or leave them blank to use the default copy which already works well. The key thing here is the **background image**. Upload a nice wide shot of your city — ideally 1920×1080. Drone shots, skylines, or iconic landmarks work great.

*Action: Show clicking "Upload Image", selecting a file*

### Sponsor Banner

*Show: Sponsor Banner card*

> If you have a launch sponsor — maybe a local business association, a co-working space, or a media partner — you can add their branding here. Toggle it on, add their name, tagline, and logo. This appears at the bottom of the landing page. It adds credibility and can help fund your launch costs.

*Action: Show toggling on, typing a sponsor name*

### Supporters Section

*Show: Supporters card*

> Similar concept — **Supporters** shows a row of logos from local organisations that back your launch. "Proudly supported by..." with their logos. Add as many as you like.

### Founding Member Counter

*Show: Founding Member Counter card*

> The **Founding Member Counter** adds urgency. Toggle it on and set the total number of spots. The landing page will show "Only X spots left!" based on how many businesses have already claimed. This drives sign-ups — people don't want to miss out.

*Action: Show toggling on, entering "150"*

### Featured Businesses

*Show: Featured Businesses card*

> **Featured Businesses** lets you hand-pick which businesses appear on your landing page. Once you've imported and approved some businesses (which we'll cover in later episodes), you can select them here. Leave this off for now if you haven't imported any yet.

### Pass Holder Count

*Show: Pass Holder Count card*

> Finally, **Pass Holder Count** — this shows "Join X people already exploring [city]" on the landing page. Social proof. Toggle it on once you have some pass holders — it looks better when the number isn't zero.

*Action: Click "Save Landing Page Configuration"*

> Save your changes, and let's see the result.
